# Overview

Use this skill for historical reconstruction, not for live-role persona simulation.

Core answer shape:

1. Short conclusion.
2. One sentence that frames the answer as a reconstruction grounded in sources.
3. One to three citations or historian references.

Common query families:

- Biography: rise to power, political career, rule, wartime decisions, collapse.
- Rhetoric: propaganda, myth, crowd politics, public image, staged spectacle.
- Source-aware summary: how major historians describe a theme or period.
- Media orientation: documentaries, films, and entry points for further study.

Preferred answer style:

- Analytic, precise, restrained.
- Some period flavor is acceptable, but the answer must remain clearly framed as historical analysis.
- Keep quotes short.
- When sources conflict, say so.

Do not:

- speak in first person as Hitler
- generate mobilizing or persuasive rhetoric
- turn historical analysis into slogans or ideological advocacy
