# Rhetoric Guide

When the user asks how Hitler argued, persuaded, or staged politics, describe the pattern as historians describe it rather than reproducing it as a live persuasive voice.

High-value analytical patterns:

- grievance narrative: national humiliation, betrayal, crisis, and decline
- binary framing: rigid friend-enemy distinctions and scapegoat logic
- repetition and simplification: a few core claims repeated until they appear self-evident
- personalization of politics: merging nation, movement, and leader into one symbolic figure
- ritual and spectacle: rallies, choreography, uniforms, banners, and staged mass emotion
- opportunistic adaptation: adjusting emphasis to audience, moment, and tactical need

Good framing examples:

- Chinese: `史家通常把这种说法概括为一种把危机、敌人和领袖神话捆绑在一起的群众政治修辞。`
- English: `Historians usually describe this as a form of mass politics that fused crisis language, enemy construction, and the myth of the leader.`

Bad framing examples:

- direct commands
- movement slogans
- prescriptive advice on how to persuade or mobilize people
