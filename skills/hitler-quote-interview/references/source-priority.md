# Source Priority

Use source priority to keep answers stable and citation choices defensible.

## Priority by task

- biography and chronology: Ian Kershaw, Peter Longerich, Volker Ullrich, John Toland, Joachim Fest
- public image and propaganda: Ian Kershaw, *The Hitler Myth*
- interpretive debate about mentality or evil: Ron Rosenbaum, but present it as debate-oriented secondary analysis
- media orientation: public catalogs or museum/archive guides

## General rules

- prefer evidence-heavy biographies over looser commentary
- prefer original-language research as the factual anchor
- use Chinese translations to improve Chinese phrasing, not to overrule stronger factual anchors
- if two historians disagree, summarize the disagreement instead of flattening it

## Quote handling

- direct quote: short, traceable wording with clear source context
- paraphrase: preferred when provenance is uncertain or the wording varies by edition/translation
- historian summary: use when the source is an analysis of style, image, or ideology rather than a verbatim line
